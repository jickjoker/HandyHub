Decimal Odds

Decimal odds represent the total return for every 1 unit wagered, including your original stake. For example, decimal odds of 3.50 mean that every $1 wagered returns $3.50 in total ($2.50 profit + $1.00 stake).



How to Convert Decimal Odds to Fractional Odds

Subtract 1 from the decimal odds.

Place the result over 1 and simplify until both the numerator (top) and denominator (bottom) are whole numbers.



Example: Converting 4.50 to Fractional Odds

4.5 - 1 = 3.5
Result: 3.5 / 1
Multiply both by 2 to get whole numbers:
3.5 x 2 = 7   and    1 x 2 = 2.
Fractional Odds: 7/2

Note: UK bookmakers do not display decimals within fractions. This is why you must multiply both numbers by the same value until they are both whole integers.



How to Convert Decimal Odds to Positive American Odds

Use this formula for decimal odds of 2.00 or higher.



Formula: (Decimal Odds - 1) x 100
Example: Converting 3.50 to American Odds

• (3.5 - 1) x 100 = 2.5 x 100
• American Odds: +250


How to Convert Decimal Odds to Negative American Odds

Use this formula for decimal odds below 2.00.



Formula: -100 / (Decimal Odds - 1)
Example: Converting 1.50 to American Odds

• 1.5 - 1 = 0.5
• -100 / 0.5 = -200
• American Odds: -200


How to Convert Decimal Odds to Implied Probability

Implied probability shows the percentage likelihood of an event happening according to the odds.



Formula: (1 / Decimal Odds) x 100
Example: Converting 5.00 to Implied Probability

• 1 / 5 = 0.20
• 0.20 x 100 = 20%
• Implied Probability:20%

Fractional Odds

Fractional odds represent the potential profit relative to your stake. For example, odds of 5/2 mean you win $5 for every $2 you wager.


How to Convert Fractional Odds to Decimal Odds

To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the top number by the bottom number and then add 1.



Example: Converting 5/2 to Decimal Odds

• (5 / 2) + 1 = 2.5 + 1
• Decimal Odds: 3.50


How to Convert Fractional Odds to Positive American Odds

Use this formula if the fraction is greater than 1/1 (the numerator is larger than the denominator).

Formula: (Numerator / Denominator) x 100


Example: Converting 5/2 to American Odds

• (5 / 2) x 100 = 2.5 x 100
• American Odds: +250


How to Convert Fractional Odds to Negative American Odds

Use this formula if the fraction is less than 1/1 (Odds-On). (the numerator is smaller than the denominator).

Formula: -100 / (Numerator / Denominator)


Example: Converting 1/2 to American Odds

• 1 / 2 = 0.5
• -100 / 0.5 = -200
• American Odds: -200


How to Convert Fractional Odds to Implied Probability



Implied probability shows the percentage chance of an event happening based on the odds.

Formula: Denominator / (Numerator + Denominator) x 100


Example: Converting 4/1 to Implied Probability

• 1 / (4 + 1) = 1 / 5
• 0.20 x 100 = 20%
• Implied Probability: 20%

American Odds

American odds, also known as Moneyline odds, use positive (+) and negative (-) numbers. Positive odds show the profit on a $100 stake, while negative odds show how much must be staked to win $100 in profit.



How to Convert Positive American Odds to Decimal Odds

Formula: (American Odds / 100) + 1


Example: Converting +250 to Decimal Odds

• (250 / 100) + 1 = 2.5 + 1
• Decimal Odds: 3.50


How to Convert Negative American Odds to Decimal Odds

Formula: (100 / |American Odds|) + 1


Example: Converting -500 to Decimal Odds

• (100 / 500) + 1 = 0.2 + 1
• Decimal Odds: 1.20


How to Convert Positive American Odds to Fractional Odds

Place the American odds over 100 and simplify the fraction.



Example: Converting +250 to Fractional Odds

• 250 / 100
• = 2.5 / 1 Multiply both by 2 to remove the decimals: • 2.5 × 2 = 5. 1 × 2 = 2. • Simplified: 5/2


How to Convert Negative American Odds to Fractional Odds

Place 100 over the American odds (ignoring the minus sign) and simplify.


Example: Converting -500 to Fractional Odds

• 100 / 500
• Simplified: 1/5


How to Convert Positive American Odds to Implied Probability

Formula: 100 / (American Odds + 100) x 100


Example: Converting +900 to Implied Probability

• 100 / (900 + 100) = 100 / 1000 = 0.1
• 0.1 x 100 = 10%


How to Convert Negative American Odds to Implied Probability

Formula: |American Odds| / (|American Odds| + 100) x 100


Example: Converting -350 to Implied Probability

• 350 / (350 + 100) = 350 / 450 = 0.7777...
• 0.7777 x 100 = 77.8%
