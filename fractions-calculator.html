How Fractions Work

A fraction represents a division between two numbers: the top number (numerator) divided by the bottom number (denominator).

  • Top number = numerator
  • Bottom number = denominator

How to Divide Fractions


Example:   35 ÷ 23

  • Step 1: Keep the first fraction the same
  • Step 2: Flip the second fraction (reciprocal)  2/3 ➡ 3/2
  • Step 4: Multiply the fractions:  3/5 × 3/2 = 9/10
  • Step 5: Simplify if possible



Result: 35 ÷ 23 = 910



How to Add Fractions


Example:   14 + 16

  • Step 1: Find a common denominator:  6 × 4 = 24
  • Step 2: Convert both fractions:  6/24 + 4/24
  • Step 3: Add the top numbers:  10/24
  • Step 4: Simplify if possible:  5/12


Result: 14 + 16 = 512
