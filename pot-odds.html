Pot Odds Explained





    

        What are pot odds?

		

        Pot odds compare the cost of calling a bet to the amount of money you can win in the pot. 
        They are one of the most important concepts in poker because they help you evaluate risk versus reward.
    


    

  

    

        Example:

		

        The pot is $5 and your opponent bets $1.
    


    
    
        
  • Current pot (including the bet):  $6
    • 
		
    
        
  • Cost to call  $1
    • 
		
    
        
  • Risk/Reward:  You are risking  $1  to win  $6.
    • 
		
    
        
  • Pot odds: 6 : 1     or   6/1
    • 
    



    





    

        Why pot odds matter:
    


    
    
        
  • Helps you decide if a call is mathematically profitable.
    • 
		
    
        
  • Improves decision making using probability instead of emotion.
    • 
		
    
        
  • They allow you to compare your winning chances against the price you are receiving.
    • 
    



    

	





    
Break-Even Percentage



    

        The break-even percentage tells you the minimum amount of the time you must win for a call to neither make nor lose money long term.
    


    

	

The Break-even Formula:



(Your Call) / (Total Pot + Your call) = Break-even %
    


    



    

        Example Calculation:
    


    
    
        
  • Current Pot = $20
    • 
        
  • Opponent Bet = $5
    • 
        
  • Your Call = $5
    • 
        
  • Total Pot = $30
    • 
    


	


The total pot including your call: 






$20 + $5 + $5  = $30.






Divide your call into the total pot: 






$5 / $30 = Break-even 16.67%
   


    
    


    

        If your chance of winning is higher than 16.67%, calling is profitable in the long run.
    



    



    
Expected Value Proof



    

        At exactly 16.67% equity, the call breaks even over time.
    





WINS:
	   





     

       16.67% of the time you win $25
	   

	   

	   

	   Win =  0.1667  ×  $25  =  + $4.16.
	   

	   

	   

	   

	   

LOSSES:
	   



 

        83.33% of the time you lose $5
    

 



        Lose =  0.833  ×  $5  =  - $4.16.
		

		

     

	 

	 

RESULT:
	   

	   

	   

          $4.16    -  $4.16  ≈  0
   


    


    

        This means the call neither wins nor loses money on average.

       How to use the cheat sheet:
    






        If the pot is $300 and your opponent bets $100  (1/3 pot):
		

		

		

		Total Pot: $300 + $100 = $400
		

		

		

		Your Call: $100
		

		

		

		Math: $100 / $400 = 20%
		

		

		

		A 1/3 pot bet always gives you 4:1 pot odds, meaning your break-even percentage is 20%.
