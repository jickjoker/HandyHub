What is ROI?

ROI (Return on Investment) is a financial metric used to measure the profitability and efficiency of an investment.

It compares the amount gained or lost relative to the original investment amount.

A higher ROI generally means a more profitable investment.




The Formula

ROI is calculated by dividing your net profit by your initial investment, then multiplying by 100.


(Total Return − Initial Investment)
Initial Investment
ROI × 100



Example

You play 300 $5 Sit & Go poker tournaments.

  • Total Buy-ins: 300 × $5 = $1500
  • Final Return: $1765
  • Profit: $1765 − $1500 = $265
ROI =
265
1500
× 100 = 17.7%



Understanding the Results

Positive ROI: Your investment made a profit.

Negative ROI: Your investment lost money.

0% ROI: You broke even with no profit or loss.




Common Uses of ROI

  • Investing: Comparing stocks, crypto, or property investments.
  • Business: Measuring product or marketing profitability.
  • Poker & Gambling: Tracking long-term profitability.
  • Sports Betting: Measuring betting efficiency over time.
  • Personal Finance: Evaluating savings or side projects.
